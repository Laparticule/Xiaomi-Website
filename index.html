China's 'Apple'

It is well known that Xiaomi designs have always resembled in some way Apple's but now, Xiaomi could very well overtake the famous brand. In fact, Strategy Analytics says the "Chinese phoenix" could become in 2018 the world's second-largest smartphone vendor, behind Samsung.

The Rise of Xiaomi

Created in 2010, Xiaomi made its mark by selling cheap smartphones directly to consumers online. The company touted blockbuster sales numbers and even set a Guinness World Record in 2015. However, selling inexpensive phones ultimately associated the brand with being cheap and not chic... This was until its 2017 comeback.

Q&A   Ask a question


Where can I buy Xiaomi products?

While there are no official Xiaomi stores in Europe, you can still import their products from China. To see what's available, you can visit Aliexpress, Amazon and Gearbest.

Do the latest Xiaomi phones work with 4G/LTE networks in France?

Three frequency bands are used for 4G LTE in France : 800 MHz, 1800 MHz and 2,6 GHz. However, Xiaomi phones are usually not compatible with 800 MHz bands. As a result, SFR and Orange clients will have difficulties accessing 4G networks in France. Free Mobile and Bouygues Telecom clients will not be impacted. Hopefully, you can now access 3G networks regardless of your operator. Read more here.

How does Xiaomi keep prices so low?

Xiaomi keeps prices competitive by selling directly to consumers via its website, butting out the middleman and removing 20 to 30 percent of the gross margin that typically goes to retail channels.
